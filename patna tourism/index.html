Spiritual Places in Patna

Mahavir Mandir

Hanuman Mandir, located near Patna Junction, is one of the most revered temples dedicated to Lord Hanuman...

Hanuman Mandir, located near Patna Junction, is one of the most revered temples dedicated to Lord Hanuman. Devotees visit daily to seek blessings, believing in the deity's power to remove obstacles. The temple is especially crowded on Tuesdays and Saturdays.

Key Features:

  • Famous for its religious significance
  • Devotees offer prayers, sweets, and vermillion
  • Spiritual aura attracts visitors year-round

Best Time to Visit: Early morning or evening

Location: Near Patna Junction

Patan Devi Mandir

Patan Devi, located in Patna, is one of the most popular temples dedicated to Goddess Durga...

Patan Devi, located in Patna, is one of the most popular temples dedicated to Goddess Durga. This famous temple is a symbol of faith and devotion, where devotees come with their wishes, believing the goddess fulfills them.

Key Features:

  • Considered a Shakti Peetha
  • Right thigh of Goddess Sati is said to have fallen here
  • Special rituals during Navratri

Best Time to Visit: During Navratri festival

Location: Patna City area

Takht Shri Harimandir Ji

TAKHAT SRI HARIMANDIR JI PATNA SAHIB is considered the second holiest Takhat...

TAKHAT SRI HARIMANDIR JI PATNA SAHIB is considered the second holiest Takhat. Revered as the birthplace of Sri Guru Gobind Singh Ji Maharaj, it is one of the five seats of temporal authority of the Sikhs.

Key Features:

  • Consecrated by three Sikh Gurus
  • Important pilgrimage site for Sikhs
  • Beautiful golden architecture

Best Time to Visit: Morning hours

Location: Patna City

Khajpura Shiv Mandir, located in Patna, is a well-known temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. This sacred place is a center of faith and devotion, attracting devotees who come to offer prayers and seek blessings.

Key Features:

  • Special rituals during Mahashivratri
  • Devotees offer milk, bilva leaves, and water to the Shivling
  • Believed to bring peace and prosperity

Best Time to Visit: Mahashivratri festival

Location: Khajpura area

ISKCON Temple

ISKCON Temple, located in Patna, is one of the most popular temples dedicated to Lord Krishna...

ISKCON Temple, located in Patna, is one of the most popular temples dedicated to Lord Krishna. This famous temple is a symbol of devotion and spirituality, where devotees come with their wishes, believing that chanting the Hare Krishna mantra brings peace and blessings.

  • Peaceful spiritual atmosphere
  • Vegetarian food offerings

Maa Siddheshwari Kali Mandir

Historical Places in Patna

Golghar

Iconic beehive-shaped granary built in 1786 after the great famine, offering panoramic views of Patna...

Built by Captain John Garstin for the British Army, this 29-meter tall structure was designed to store grains for famine relief but was never filled to capacity.

Key Features:

  • 125-step spiral staircase to the top
  • Walls 3.6m thick at the base
  • No pillars support the structure
  • Unique ventilation system

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: ₹15 for adults
  • Timings: 10AM-5PM (Closed Mondays)
  • Best Time: Early morning for sunrise views
Agam Kuan

Ancient "Unfathomable Well" believed to be part of Emperor Ashoka's torture chambers...

This 105-foot deep well is said to be part of Ashoka's torture chambers before he embraced Buddhism, later becoming a sacred site.

Key Features:

  • Mentioned in Chinese traveler Hiuen Tsang's accounts
  • Eight arched windows at ground level
  • Adjacent shrines from different periods
  • Local legends about mystical properties

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Timings: 6AM-6PM daily
  • Location: Near Gulzarbagh
Patna Museum

Established in 1917, houses artifacts from ancient Pataliputra including the famous Didarganj Yakshi...

Known locally as 'Jadu Ghar', the museum contains over 50,000 artifacts dating back to 3rd century BCE from the Mauryan and Gupta periods.

Key Features:

  • Didarganj Yakshi (3rd century BCE sculpture)
  • Ashes of Buddha in a casket
  • Rare coins and paintings collection
  • Traditional handicrafts display

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: ₹100 (foreigners), ₹15 (Indians)
  • Timings: 10:30AM-4:30PM (Closed Mondays)
  • Location: Buddha Marg
Gandhi Maidan

Historic 60-acre ground where Mahatma Gandhi held rallies during India's freedom struggle...

This historic ground has witnessed many important political events and continues to host major gatherings and festivals.

Key Features:

  • Tallest Gandhi statue in India (70 feet)
  • Hosts Republic Day parade annually
  • Main venue for Chhath Puja celebrations
  • Central location in Patna

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Best Time: During festivals
  • Location: Heart of Patna
Bihar Museum

State-of-the-art museum showcasing Bihar's heritage through interactive displays and galleries...

Opened in 2017, this modern museum spans 13.5 acres and presents Bihar's 2000+ years of history through engaging exhibits.

Key Features:

  • Didarganj Yakshi (transferred from Patna Museum)
  • Prehistoric tools and fossils
  • Children's discovery room
  • Digital interactive displays

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: ₹100 (adults), ₹50 (children)
  • Timings: 10:30AM-5PM (Closed Mondays)
  • Location: Jawaharlal Nehru Marg
Sabhyata Dwar

32-meter tall sandstone monument celebrating Bihar's cultural heritage, inaugurated in 2018...

This gateway symbolizes Bihar's golden age during Mauryan and Gupta periods, built with sandstone from Chunar.

Key Features:

  • Inspired by ancient Indian architecture
  • Inscriptions of Bihar's historical achievements
  • Beautiful night lighting effects
  • Gateway to Patna's riverfront

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Best Time: Evening for light show
  • Location: Ganga Riverfront
Kargil Chowk

War memorial dedicated to soldiers who sacrificed their lives in the 1999 Kargil War...

Established in 2000 near Gandhi Maidan, this memorial stands as a symbol of patriotism and honors the Kargil War heroes.

Key Features:

  • Plagues with names of fallen soldiers
  • Patriotic architecture and design
  • Site for national day commemorations
  • Emotional and inspiring landmark

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Best Time: Republic Day/Independence Day
  • Location: Near Gandhi Maidan
Maner Sharif

Ancient Sufi shrine complex 30km from Patna with stunning Mughal architecture...

This important Sufi pilgrimage site contains the tombs of Makhdoom Yahya Maneri and Makhdoom Shah Daulat, built in Mughal architectural style.

Key Features:

  • Beautiful Mughal-era mausoleums
  • Center of Islamic learning historically
  • Famous for "Maner Ka Ladoo" sweets
  • Peaceful spiritual atmosphere

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Timings: 6AM-8PM daily
  • Distance: 30km west of Patna

Famous Foods in Patna

Litti Chokha

Traditional Bihari dish of wheat balls stuffed with sattu, served with mashed spiced vegetables...

The signature dish of Bihar, Litti Chokha was originally the staple food of warriors and is now popular across India.

Key Features:

  • Made with whole wheat dough and sattu (roasted gram flour) filling
  • Traditionally baked over cow dung cakes
  • Served with baingan (eggplant) or alu (potato) chokha
  • Often accompanied by ghee, pickle and raw onions

Where to Try:

  • Best Places: Pandit Ji Litti Chokha, Boring Road; Anand Litti Stall, Patna City
  • Price Range: ₹50-100 per plate
  • Best Time: Available all day, but best for lunch
Khaja

Flaky, layered sweet pastry made from wheat flour, sugar and ghee, a specialty of Bihar...

This crispy dessert has been part of Bihari cuisine for centuries and is often associated with religious offerings.

Key Features:

  • Multiple thin layers of dough fried in ghee
  • Soaked in sugar syrup after frying
  • Comes in different varieties - soft, hard and liquid-filled
  • Specialty of Silao near Nalanda

Where to Try:

  • Best Places: Harilal Sweet Shop, Patna City; Ramana Sweets, Boring Road
  • Price Range: ₹200-500 per kg
  • Best Time: Available year-round
Chana Ghugni

Spicy chickpea curry served as a popular street food snack, especially during winters...

A protein-packed snack that's both delicious and nutritious, often eaten with puffed rice or bread.

Key Features:

  • Made with black chickpeas or yellow peas
  • Cooked with onions, garlic and special spices
  • Garnished with chopped onions, coriander and lemon
  • Often served with muri (puffed rice) or bread

Where to Try:

  • Best Places: Street vendors near Gandhi Maidan; Maurya Lok Complex
  • Price Range: ₹20-50 per plate
  • Best Time: Evening snack (4-7 PM)
Malpua

Sweet pancake dessert soaked in sugar syrup, often served with rabri during festivals...

A traditional sweet that's especially popular during Holi and other festivals in Bihar.

Key Features:

  • Made with flour, milk, bananas and fennel seeds
  • Deep fried and then dipped in sugar syrup
  • Often served with thickened milk (rabri)
  • Variations include coconut or semolina malpua

Where to Try:

  • Best Places: Hari Om Sweets, Fraser Road; Kankarbagh Sweet House
  • Price Range: ₹30-80 per piece
  • Best Time: Winter months and festivals
Thekua

Traditional cookie-like snack made during Chhath Puja from wheat flour, jaggery and coconut...

A sacred offering during Chhath Puja that has become popular as a tea-time snack throughout the year.

Key Features:

  • Made with whole wheat flour, jaggery and ghee
  • Often flavored with cardamom or fennel
  • Pressed into decorative molds before frying
  • Has good shelf life of 2-3 weeks

Where to Try:

  • Best Places: Local sweet shops during Chhath; homemade versions are best
  • Price Range: ₹200-400 per kg
  • Best Time: Chhath Puja season (Oct-Nov)
Buniya Sev

Crunchy gram flour snack, a Patna specialty often enjoyed with tea or as a quick bite...

This savory snack is unique to Patna and different from regular sev found elsewhere in India.

Key Features:

  • Made from besan (gram flour) dough
  • Pressed through a sieve and deep-fried
  • Seasoned with spices and salt
  • Light, crispy texture with mild spices

Where to Try:

  • Best Places: Local snack shops in Patna City; Buniya stalls near Dak Bungalow
  • Price Range: ₹100-200 per kg
  • Best Time: Available year-round
Dal Pitha

Bihari version of dumplings, made with rice flour and stuffed with spiced lentil paste...

Often called Bihari momos, these steamed or fried dumplings are a traditional comfort food.

Key Features:

  • Outer covering made from rice flour
  • Stuffed with spiced chana dal paste
  • Can be steamed or pan-fried
  • Often served with chutney or yogurt

Where to Try:

  • Best Places: Traditional Bihari thali restaurants; homemade versions
  • Price Range: ₹50-100 per plate
  • Best Time: Winter months
Tilkut

Sesame seed and jaggery sweet, especially popular during Makar Sankranti festival...

This nutritious sweet is a winter specialty and is believed to provide warmth during cold months.

Key Features:

  • Made with sesame seeds and jaggery
  • Pounded to create a crunchy texture
  • Often shaped into round discs or bars
  • Rich in iron and calcium

Where to Try:

  • Best Places: Gaya is famous for Tilkut; sweet shops in Patna during winter
  • Price Range: ₹300-600 per kg
  • Best Time: December-January
Anarsa

Traditional sweet made from rice flour and jaggery, with a crisp exterior and soft interior...

A festival sweet that requires days of preparation, known for its unique texture and flavor.

Key Features:

  • Made with soaked rice, jaggery and poppy seeds
  • Requires 3-4 days of fermentation
  • Crispy on outside, soft and porous inside
  • Traditional Diwali sweet in Bihar

Where to Try:

  • Best Places: Traditional sweet shops during festivals
  • Price Range: ₹400-700 per kg
  • Best Time: Diwali season

Cultural Attractions in Patna

Chhath Puja

Ancient Hindu festival dedicated to the Sun God, celebrated with fasting and river rituals...

Chhath Puja is Bihar's most important festival, celebrated with great devotion over four days in November.

Key Features:

  • Four days of rigorous rituals (Nahay Khay to Usha Arghya)
  • Devotees offer prayers to rising and setting sun
  • Holy dips in Ganges with offerings
  • Traditional folk songs and vibrant processions

Visitor Information:

  • Best Places to Experience: Gandhi Ghat, Digha Ghat, Patna Marine Drive
  • Best Time: 6 days after Diwali (Nov 7-10 in 2024)
  • Duration: 4 days
Diwali

The festival of lights transforms Patna into a glittering wonderland with lamps, fireworks, and sweets...

Patna celebrates Diwali with grand illuminations, especially along the Ganga ghats and markets like Patna Market.

Key Features:

  • Gandhi Ghat illuminated with thousands of diyas
  • Firework displays over the Ganges
  • Special Lakshmi Puja in homes and businesses
  • Sweet shops offer seasonal specialties like Anarsa and Kheel

Best Places to Experience:

  • Gandhi Ghat (diyas floating on Ganges)
  • Patna Market (decorations shopping)
  • ISKCON Temple (special aarti)

When: October/November

Ram Navami

Birthday celebrations of Lord Rama featuring grand processions and devotional music...

Patna observes Ram Navami with week-long celebrations culminating in chariot processions.

Key Features:

  • Rath Yatra (chariot procession) from Patna City to Gandhi Maidan
  • 24-hour Ramayana recitals
  • Traditional folk performances
  • Community feasts (Bhandara)

Best Places to Experience:

  • Ram Janki Temple (Patna City)
  • Gandhi Maidan (main procession)
  • Buddha Colony (cultural programs)

When: March/April (Chaitra month)

Govardhan Puja

Celebration of Lord Krishna's miracle with mountain-shaped food offerings...

Celebrated the day after Diwali, this festival features elaborate food mountains honoring Krishna's protection.

Key Features:

  • Annakut (56+ food items arranged as a mountain)
  • Cow worship (Gau Puja)
  • Community feasts at temples
  • Folk dramas depicting Krishna's life

Best Places to Experience:

  • ISKCON Patna (grandest Annakut display)
  • Patna Sahib Gurudwara (special langar)
  • Local neighborhoods with family celebrations

When: Day after Diwali

Makar Sankranti

Harvest festival marking sun's transition into Capricorn, celebrated with kite flying and sweets...

This festival in mid-January celebrates the end of winter and beginning of the harvest season.

Key Features:

  • Massive kite flying competitions
  • Holy dips in Ganges
  • Distribution of tilkut, chura and dahi
  • Traditional folk songs and dances

Visitor Information:

  • When: January 14-15 annually
  • Best Places: Gandhi Ghat, banks of Ganges
  • Entry Fee: Free
Durga Puja

Grand celebration of Goddess Durga's victory over evil, with elaborate pandals across the city...

The Bengali community's biggest festival has become a major cultural event in Patna.

Key Features:

  • Artistically designed pandals (temporary shrines)
  • Cultural programs and competitions
  • Traditional dhak drum performances
  • Procession for idol immersion

Visitor Information:

  • When: September-October annually
  • Best Pandals: SK Puri, Boring Road, Patna City
  • Duration: 5 days

Recreation Spots in Patna

Eco Park

Sprawling 400-acre green space with walking trails, boating, and children's play areas...

Patna's largest urban park offering a refreshing escape with well-maintained gardens and recreational facilities.

Key Features:

  • Paddle boats and row boats on the lake
  • Open-air gym equipment
  • Musical fountain show in evenings
  • Dedicated children's play zone

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: ₹20 per person
  • Timings: 6AM-9PM daily
  • Best Time: Early morning or evening
  • Location: J.P. Ganga Path
Ganga Path

Scenic 5km riverfront promenade along the Ganges, perfect for walking and cycling...

Patna's Marine Drive offers breathtaking views of the Ganges with well-lit pathways and seating areas.

Key Features:

  • Beautiful sunset views over the river
  • Cycling track and jogging path
  • Food stalls serving local snacks
  • Regular cultural events

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Timings: 24 hours (best 5-8AM & 4-8PM)
  • Facilities: Bicycle rentals available
Buddha Smriti Park

Peaceful memorial park dedicated to Lord Buddha, featuring a grand stupa and museum...

Inaugurated by the Dalai Lama in 2010, this park commemorates Buddha's connection with Patna.

Key Features:

  • Pataliputra Karuna Stupa (200ft tall)
  • Sacred Bodhi tree sapling
  • Meditation cells and prayer hall
  • Museum with Buddhist relics

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: ₹50 for adults
  • Timings: 9AM-7PM daily
  • Special: Guided meditation sessions
Sanjay Gandhi Biological Park

Patna's zoo and botanical garden spread over 150 acres with diverse flora and fauna...

Established in 1969, this is one of the largest zoos in India with over 800 animals.

Key Features:

  • White tiger and lion safari
  • Aquarium and reptile house
  • Butterfly park and orchid house
  • Toy train for children

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: ₹50 for adults, ₹20 for children
  • Timings: 8AM-5PM (Closed Mondays)
  • Best Time: October-March
NIT Ghat

Serene riverside area near NIT Patna, popular for morning walks and evening relaxation...

This well-maintained ghat offers peaceful views of the Ganges away from the city crowds.

Key Features:

  • Clean and less crowded riverside
  • Yoga and meditation spot
  • Small food stalls serving tea/snacks
  • Boat rides available

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Timings: Open 24 hours
  • Best Time: Sunrise or sunset
Patna Planetarium

One of Asia's largest planetariums offering fascinating astronomy shows and exhibits...

Built in 1993, this dome-shaped planetarium is a great educational and recreational spot.

Key Features:

  • State-of-the-art projection system
  • Regular shows on astronomy
  • Science exhibition gallery
  • Telescope viewing sessions

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: ₹50 for adults, ₹25 for children
  • Show Timings: 12:30PM, 2PM, 3:30PM, 5PM
  • Closed: Mondays
Patna Golf Club

18-hole golf course set along the banks of the Ganges, established during British era...

Founded in 1916, this is one of the oldest golf courses in India with scenic river views.

Key Features:

  • 6,200 yard, par 72 course
  • Clubhouse with restaurant
  • Golf coaching available
  • Regular tournaments

Visitor Information:

  • Entry: Members only (temporary passes available)
  • Timings: 6AM-6PM daily
  • Facilities: Equipment rentals available
Water Park

Fun-filled aquatic park with thrilling water slides and pools for all age groups...

Patna's premier water-based entertainment destination, especially popular in summers.

Key Features:

  • Wave pool and lazy river
  • High-speed water slides
  • Children's play area
  • Rain dance floor

Visitor Information:

  • Entry Fee: ₹500-800 per person
  • Timings: 10AM-6PM (April-September)
  • Facilities: Lockers and food court available
